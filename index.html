The Web Design Industry

Web developers create and maintain websites. Digital designers develop, create, and test website or interface layout, functions, and navigation for usability. Some web developers and digital designers work in the computer systems design and related services industry. Others are self-employed. Still others work in industries including publishing, management consulting, and advertising.

Why Web Design?

What is it?

Web developers create and maintain websites. Digital designers develop, create, and test website or interface layout, functions, and navigation for usability.

Relevance?

One of the fastest growing industries, expected to increase by 8% over the next 10 years (2019-2029), which is much faster than everage

Salary?

Median pay: $73,760/ year or $36.46/hour

Qualifications?

2-year associates degree, as well as good communication skills, creativity, customer service skills, problem solving skills, and being detail-oriented

Importance

Web designers assist companies by creating user-friendly and appealing websites and mobile apps. This has made them indispensable in the business environment today because a company’s online presence makes a big difference in its bottom line.

Integration

Integration is an important aspect of web design and development

Optimization

Optimization is an important aspect of web design and development

Website optimization is the process of using tools, advanced strategies, and experiments to improve the performance of your website, further drive more traffic, increase conversions, and grow revenue.

Testing

Testing is an important aspect of web design and development

Website software testing removes the hurdles that can affect your website ranking, boosts its interactivity through optimal design and makes it easier to backlink

Education

What education is required to become a web designer?

Associates

This is a 2-year degree and it good for starting out and is the fastest track to the work force. However, in most cases it will not allow you to make competative wages.

Bachelor

This is the most common degree held by web designers as it allows you to earn competative wages and be competative in the job market.

Masters

This degree will allow you to make high-end wages.

PhD

Most people who hold a PhD in Web Design become proffessors in the field and also get to pursue their passion for Web Design part time. On average, they make the highest salaries. As the industry is rapidly growing, there is a bigger and bigger demand for professors in this field
